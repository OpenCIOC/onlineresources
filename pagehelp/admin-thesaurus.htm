Create New Term

To create a new Subject Term, click the Create new Subject Term link. You will be taken to a form to enter the term information.

Manage Source Values

Each Subject Term can be associated with a Source (i.e. "InformOntario"). To edit the available list of Source values, click the Manage Thesaurus Source Values link.

Edit existing Term

To edit an existing Subject Term, use the Search form to locate the Term(s) you want to edit. If you do not enter any criteria default, the search form defaults to returning all Active Terms.

Keyword Search Tips

You may search in the French or English Term names or Notes fields. The search form defaults to searching the English Term name.

You may choose to match All the Terms or phrases in your list, Any one of the terms or phrases, or you may use the Boolean option to create a custom search. Note that the system is unable to correct badly formed Boolean search phrases, and errors in a Boolean search will yield no results. When creating a Boolean search, all terms or phrases must be connected with "and", "or"; you may also preference any term or phrase with the word "not". Round Brackets may be used to group sets of terms or phrases together.

You may perform two simultaneous keyword searches of the Thesaurus. If you perform two keyword searches, you must specify how to join the two:

  • AND - Both search criteria must match
  • OR - Either search criteria must match
  • AND NOT - The first criteria must match, and the second criteria must not match
  • OR NOT - The first criteria must match, or the second criteria must not match

Changing the Results Display

At the bottom of the search form, you will see options for changing the display of your search results.

You may sort the list of terms by the Name of the Term (English or French, depending on your current display language), or by the Last Modified date of the term with most recently modified Terms appearing first.

By default, the results display will include:

  • The Term name (in the current language)
  • The preferred Term (if the term is Unused, which Term to use instead)
  • The number of records using the subject (The count is for All records in the database, including those not in the current View)

By selected Display Full Subject Information, you will see all available information about the subject including:

  • Broader, Narrower and Related Terms
  • Used For information - where the given Term is a preferred Term for another, Unused Term.
  • Data management information.
