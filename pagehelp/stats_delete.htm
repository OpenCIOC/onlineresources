Note: You may find the page help for the main statistics search form useful for a general overview of the different types of statistics that are kept for your CIOC database and how to use of them appropriately.

It is recommended that you periodically clear the database of old Record View Statistics that you do not require anymore, particularly if you work in a database that has high traffic (i.e. more than 250,000 record views per month). You should select an appropriate cut-off date for removing old statistics, such as your previous year-end for reporting to funders. Note that, as part of your membership commitment with CIOC, you must keep a minimum of 6 months statistics at all times; this information is used for the calculation of membership fees.

Before Deleting

  • Make sure you have a full backup/download of both your statistics and data (these are kept in separate files). Having a full data backup will allow you to reproduce statistical reports offline if it were required.
  • Consult any and all data partners and confirm the plan to remove old statistics. Statistics removal is not confined to any particular View or Agency, it will remove all statistics in the given module up to the period of the selected date.
  • Save and/or print any statistical reports you think you might want for the time segment you are deleting.

Deleting the Statistics

On this page there is a table with two columns; the column on the left is the "Delete Up To and Including" column, with dates and radio buttons for selecting a date. The column on the right lists the number of statistics that would be deleted if you select that particular date.

To delete stats, click in the radio button for the month that you want to select. This will delete all stats for the selected month, and everything prior to that month. So if you selected March 2009, then all stats that occurred during or before March 2009 would be permanently deleted.

Important Note: once statistics have been deleted, they are permanently deleted and can not be restored without a backup and the assistance of a system administrator (at your cost). It is a good idea to keep a backup of your daily download, so that if you ever want to refer to older statistics you will have them in your download file.
